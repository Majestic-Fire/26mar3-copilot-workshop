# Cooking Methods Reference

A guide to the 10 cooking methods used in this skill's demo recipes.

---

## 1. Grilling

- **Heat source:** Direct flame or radiant heat from below
- **Best for:** Fish, steaks, vegetables, burgers
- **Typical temp:** 200–260°C / 400–500°F
- **Key tip:** Preheat grill fully before placing food. Oil the grate, not the food.

## 2. Roasting

- **Heat source:** Dry heat in an enclosed oven, all sides
- **Best for:** Whole chicken, large cuts of meat, root vegetables
- **Typical temp:** 180–220°C / 350–425°F
- **Key tip:** Use a roasting rack so air circulates underneath.

## 3. Braising

- **Heat source:** Low oven heat with liquid in a covered pot
- **Best for:** Tough cuts (chuck, shoulder), stews
- **Typical temp:** 150–170°C / 300–340°F
- **Key tip:** Sear the meat first for flavor, then add liquid and cover.

## 4. Stir-Frying

- **Heat source:** Very high direct heat in a wok
- **Best for:** Thinly sliced vegetables, small pieces of protein
- **Typical temp:** 230–260°C / 450–500°F
- **Key tip:** All ingredients must be prepped before you start — it goes fast.

## 5. Baking

- **Heat source:** Dry oven heat, usually moderate
- **Best for:** Pasta, casseroles, bread, pastries
- **Typical temp:** 160–200°C / 325–400°F
- **Key tip:** Preheat oven fully. Use the center rack for even heat.

## 6. Pan-Searing

- **Heat source:** High direct heat from a heavy skillet (cast iron preferred)
- **Best for:** Steaks, skin-on fish, scallops
- **Typical temp:** 220–250°C / 430–480°F
- **Key tip:** Pat protein completely dry. Don't move it — let the crust form.

## 7. Steaming

- **Heat source:** Steam from boiling water below a perforated basket
- **Best for:** Dumplings, fish, vegetables
- **Typical temp:** 100°C / 212°F (steam temperature)
- **Key tip:** Don't let the water touch the food. Keep the lid on.

## 8. Deep-Frying

- **Heat source:** Full submersion in hot oil
- **Best for:** Chicken, fries, doughnuts, fritters
- **Typical temp:** 170–190°C / 340–375°F
- **Key tip:** Use a thermometer. Overcrowding drops oil temperature and makes food soggy.

## 9. Slow-Cooking

- **Heat source:** Sustained low heat over many hours
- **Best for:** Pulled pork, brisket, beans, tough cuts
- **Typical temp:** 95–130°C / 200–265°F
- **Key tip:** Don't lift the lid — each peek adds 15-20 minutes of cook time.

## 10. Poaching

- **Heat source:** Gentle simmering liquid (water, broth, wine)
- **Best for:** Eggs, chicken breast, fish, fruit
- **Typical temp:** 75–90°C / 165–195°F
- **Key tip:** No bubbles breaking the surface. If it's boiling, it's too hot.
